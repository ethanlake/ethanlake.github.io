  • Pairing symmetry of twisted bilayer graphene: a phenomenological synthesis

    tl;dr: Experiments have advanced to the point where the pairing symmetry of the superconductor in twisted bilayer graphene can likely be nearly uniquely determined on experimental grounds alone, without relying on any particular assumptions from theory. This analysis suggests that the spin part of the pairing is a mixture of singlet and triplet, and that the orbital part is either p- or d-wave, with future Andreev tunneling measurements able to distinguish between the two.
  • The dipolar Bose-Hubbard model

    tl;dr: The dynamics of systems with dipole moment conservation has been the subject of intense recent study. Comparatively little attention however has focused on the consequences of dipole conservation for equilibrium physics. In this work we study a simple dipole-conserving modification of the Bose-Hubbard model, and show that dipole conservation drastically modifies the phase diagram. In particular, the superfluid of the Bose-Hubbard model is replaced by an `Bose-Einstein insulator': a phase which has a BEC and U(1) symmetry breaking, but which is not a superfluid.
  • Topological Mott insulators and discontinuous U(1) theta terms

    tl;dr: We study a 2+1d lattice field theory which captures a series of transitions between superfluids and topological Mott insulators.
  • Spontaneous breaking of multipole symmetries

    tl;dr: We construct generalized Mermin-Wagner arguments that tell one when thermal and quantum fluctuations prevent multipole symmetries from spontaneously breaking. We also give generalized Imry-Ma arguments for the robustness of such multipolar symmetry breaking to disorder.
  • RG and stability in the exciton Bose liquid

    tl;dr: The exciton Bose liquid (EBL) is a model of bosons hopping on a square lattice, which is interesting because it exhibits UV / IR mixing: low-energy modes exist on scales all the way down to the lattice spacing. This work discusses how to think about questions relating to RG and universality in this model, and how to determine whether or not the theory can be realized without fine-tuning (answer: no for the simplest version of the theory, but probably yes for slightly more complicated versions).
  • Subdimensional criticality: condensation of lineons and planons in the X-cube model

    tl;dr: Continous phase transitions out of fracton orders exist, with the simplest examples being various condensation transitions in the X-Cube model. These condensation transitions can have either quasi-1d or quasi-2d critical exponents, and the condensed phases can be rigorously identified as various types of deconfined gauge theories.
  • Re-entrant Superconductivity Through a Quantum Lifshitz Transition in Twisted Trilayer Graphene

    tl;dr: Twisted trilayer graphene superconducts in a non-singlet way, and posseses a field-induced transition into a distinct high-field superconducting state. These observations can be explained using a combination of phenomenological and theoretical reasoning, suggesting that the high-field phase possesses finite momentum pairing and is reached via a quantum Lifshitz point.
  • Bose-Luttinger liquids

    tl;dr: Interesting bosonic versions of Fermi liquids can exist in systems with special dispersion relations. Like Fermi liquids these systems have a ``large'' number of low-energy degrees of freedom, are metallic, have an large number of emergent conservation laws, etc. Unlike Fermi liquids they are not stabilized by degeneracy pressure, do not have quasiparticles, and have correlation functions controlled by continuously-varying exponents
  • Landau ordering phase transitions beyond the Landau paradigm

    tl;dr: Even for a second-order phase transition between a Landau-ordered symmetry-breaking phase and a trivial symmetric one, Landau-Ginzburg isn't the full story. Examples exist where the transition is Landau-allowed, but has a critical point described by an emergent non-Abelian gauge theory
  • Higher-form symmetries and spontaneous symmetry breaking

    tl;dr: One can proove versions of Goldstone's theorem and the Mermin-Wagner theorem for systems with higher symmetries
  • Fermion condensation and super pivotal categories

    tl;dr: There is a systematic method of performing fermion condensation in TQFTs, and the resulting topological phases can be described mathematically by an extension of the usual MTC framework
  • Spinon magnetic resonance of quantum spin liquids

    tl;dr: Doing spin resonance experiments in systems where the electron spins fractionalize ("spinon magnetic resonance") is a good way to probe the behavior of spin-orbit-coupled quantum spin liquids
  • Fracton topological order via coupled layers

    tl;dr: Various fracton models can be constructed from 2+1D TQFTs in a simple way
  • The structure of fixed-point tensor network states characterizes their pattern of long-range entanglement

    tl;dr: The entanglement structures of certain 2D tensor networks and of 3D TQFTs can be used to understand the relation between the two
  • Anomalies and symmetry fractionalization in reflection-symmetric topological order

    tl;dr: One can use dimensional-reduction ideas to study anomaly inflow for reflection-symmetric topological orders
  • Signatures of broken parity and time reversal symmetry in generalized string-net models

    tl;dr: Tools can be developed to encorporate time-reversal symmetry breaking into the string-net graphical calculus
  • Rashba vs Kohn-Luttinger: evolution of p-wave superconductivity in magnetized two-dimensional Fermi gases subject to spin-orbit interactions

    tl;dr: Rashba SOC affects Kohn-Luttinger type two-dimensional superconductors in interesting ways, especially when they are placed in magnetic fields
  • Detecting Extrasolar Asteroid Belts Through Their Microlensing Signatures

    tl;dr: Simulations of high-redshift star forming galaxies indicate that their observed extended Lyman-alpha emission predominantly originates from the outer halo, in the form of cooling and star-forming radiation
